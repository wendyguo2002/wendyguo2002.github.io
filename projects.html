Synthetic Animal Motion Generation

Developed an end-to-end pipeline to reconstruct realistic 3D quadruped motion from monocular video, addressing the scarcity of high-quality motion data for radar-based learning systems. The system converts raw video frames into structured joint trajectories that can be used for simulation, modeling, and downstream machine learning tasks. Integrated the reconstructed motion into a Unity-based animation system to generate controllable and scalable synthetic datasets.

Tech: Python, PyTorch, OpenCV, Unity, SMAL

Problem

Real-world animal motion data is difficult and expensive to capture, especially for radar-based simulation where labeled motion sequences are limited. Existing datasets lack diversity and controllability, making it hard to train robust models.

Approach

Designed a multi-stage pipeline that begins with video decomposition into frames, followed by segmentation to extract animal silhouettes. Applied skeletonization to recover structural geometry and detect keypoints representing anatomical joints. These 2D keypoints are then used to fit a SMAL model, producing temporally consistent 3D pose sequences. The resulting motion is represented as joint trajectories and integrated into a Unity motion matching system for animation synthesis.

Key Contributions

- Designed the full video-to-3D reconstruction pipeline
- Implemented skeleton extraction and keypoint detection
- Integrated SMAL model fitting for 3D pose recovery
- Built Unity-based motion system for animation generation

Result

Successfully generated realistic quadruped motion sequences and enabled controllable animation within a simulation pipeline. This approach provides a scalable solution for synthetic motion data generation, supporting radar training and simulation tasks.
